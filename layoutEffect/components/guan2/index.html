Plastic waste

There are three main sources of plastic garbage in the ocean.

One is that storms washed plastic garbage buried on land to

In the sea, the second is that a few people in the maritime

industry lack environmental awareness and dump plastic garbage into the sea.

The third is various marine accidents. Cargo ships encounter storms at sea.

Containers on the deck fall into the sea. Will become a "rovers" at sea.

According to French newspaper Le Figaro reported on October 21, in 2013,

1.5 million animals became victims of plastic waste in the ocean.

Lawrence Morris, a member of the French Development Institute (IRD),

said the problem could continue to worsen. Marine pollution caused by plastic

waste has a huge impact on animals. In the North Pacific, 30% of fish eat plastic.

All species, including birds, whales, and turtles, are affected.

Many animals eat this plastic waste, which is deadly to them.

The toxins in plastic will eventually return to the human table.

The report pointed out that people refer to the plastic garbage

floating on the ocean as the "seventh continent."

These plastic wastes are unrecognizable objects,

including common bottle caps and plastic bags.

Human behavior and ocean currents have caused these plastic wastes to be

concentrated in the North Pacific, South Pacific, North Atlantic, South

Atlantic, and central Indian Ocean, causing pollution. It is reported that

the most concentrated area of plastic waste is in the waters between

California and Hawaii. Since its discovery in 1997, it now has an area of

3.5 million square kilometers, equivalent to seven times the size of

France. Each year, the area of this plastic waste will increase by 80,000 square kilometers.
